# Product

## Register

product

## Users

B2B professionals: refrigeration technicians, HVAC/R installers, and procurement staff at service companies in Colombia and Latin America. They arrive knowing the part they need — searching by name, SKU, or brand. Price, stock availability, and spec completeness are the decision signals. They are task-focused, time-pressured, and technically literate. The interface is a professional instrument, not a shop window.

## Product Purpose

FSP Parts is a specialized HVAC/R parts distributor offering 5,000+ products across seven technical product lines: compressors, valves, refrigerants, filters, heat exchangers, pressure gauges, and controllers. The catalog is backed by Supabase, the cart supports COP/USD pricing, and the platform includes a thermal load calculator as a value-added tool. Success means a technician can find the right part, check stock, and complete an order in under three minutes.

## Brand Personality

Technical, reliable, no-frills. Reference: RS Components, Grainger — spec-forward, catalog-clarity over design flair. The tone is confident and direct, like a technical datasheet that knows exactly what it is. Not promotional. Not cute. Not modern-SaaS.

## Anti-references

- Consumer / retail e-commerce (Amazon, MercadoLibre): too promotional, too many sale badges and impulse-buy patterns. HVAC/R buyers are rational, not browsing.
- Generic blue-orange Tailwind SaaS template: looks like a starter kit, not a specialized industrial distributor. The current codebase is close to this — it should move away.

## Design Principles

1. **Spec over spectacle.** The catalog is the product. Hierarchy, density, and scannability beat visual flair. A technician with ten minutes should find what they need without friction.
2. **Earn trust through precision.** In industrial procurement, a sloppy interface signals a sloppy supply chain. Typography, alignment, and data accuracy all communicate competence.
3. **Density without noise.** B2B users want more information per screen, not less. Embrace purposeful density — no padding inflation to fill space, no cards where a list would do.
4. **Workflow over persuasion.** This is a tool. Interactions should feel like reaching for a trusted instrument. Discount language, urgency badges, and promotional patterns belong in consumer retail.
5. **Technical vocabulary as first-class content.** SKUs, part numbers, refrigerant codes, and technical specs are not ugly metadata to be hidden — they are the primary language of the user and deserve clear typographic treatment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No hard compliance target specified. Practical approach: sufficient contrast, keyboard navigability for core flows (search, catalog, cart), and meaningful alt text on product images. Aim for WCAG AA on text contrast as a baseline.
